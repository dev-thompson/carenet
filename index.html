Health Information Center

Visual guides to common health concerns

Malaria

Common symptoms include fever, chills, headache, and fatigue appearing 10-15 days after mosquito bite.

Diagnosis: Rapid diagnostic test (RDT) or microscopy of blood sample.

Insecticide-treated nets

Indoor residual spraying

Mosquito repellents

Remove standing water

Maternity Care

Recommended schedule:

  • 1st visit: Before 12 weeks
  • 2nd visit: 24-26 weeks
  • 3rd visit: 32 weeks
  • 4th visit: 36-38 weeks

More visits if high-risk pregnancy.

Seek immediate care for:
  • Severe headache
  • Blurred vision
  • Vaginal bleeding
  • Severe abdominal pain
  • Reduced fetal movement

Child Nutrition

Critical nutrition timeline:

  • 0-6 months: Exclusive breastfeeding
  • 6-12 months: Introduce diverse foods + breastfeeding
  • 12-24 months: Family foods + continued breastfeeding

Fortified porridge

Mashed vegetables

Protein sources

Vitamin-rich fruits

Cholera Prevention

Make water safe:

  • Boil for 1 minute
  • Use chlorine tablets
  • Solar disinfection (SODIS)
  • Filter through clean cloth

Proper handwashing

Food preparation

Sanitary latrines

Waste management
